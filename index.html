Why This Matters

Foundational Behavior Models represent a paradigm shift in robotics. The goal is to create robot generalists—systems that can learn and generalize behaviors across diverse tasks and environments through multimodal learning, rather than being limited to narrow, preprogrammed actions. By teaching robots to understand and respond to language, vision, and physical interactions, we're working toward machines that can adapt to real-world complexity.

While startups, BigTech, and governments invest billions globally in this technology, Germany faces a critical research gap in this emerging field. This bootcamp catalyzes collaborative research across the German robotics community, positioning Germany at the forefront of this transformation and establishing leadership in technology that will reshape manufacturing, healthcare, and robotics.

About KCIST

The KIT Center Information · Systems · Technologies (KCIST) brings together leading researchers across computer science, robotics, and AI at Karlsruhe Institute of Technology. KIT researchers have demonstrated that European labs can compete at the highest level, with recent publications at CoRL 2025 and NeurIPS 2025 on Vision-Language-Action models that go beyond fine-tuning—proving that thoughtful design can outperform resource-intensive approaches from billion-dollar labs.

This success challenges the misconception that Europe only contributes regulatory restrictions to AI. KCIST is actively establishing that Europe innovates, setting a new standard for what European research can achieve in foundational robotics AI.

What We're Looking For

Research-Ready PhD Candidates

We seek projects that combine your existing expertise with foundation models to advance capabilities. Bring your domain knowledge in control, perception, manipulation, planning, or any robotics subfield and explore how foundation models can advance your research—or how your expertise can advance foundation models.

Requirements

Strong implementation skills: Prepared to derive methods, implement prototypes, and run rigorous experiments.

Concrete research direction: Demonstrate that you can start immediately with a project scoped for completion at your home university within weeks after the bootcamp.

Home advisor support: Your advisor must commit to supporting project completion and publication submission.

Methodological innovation: We seek projects that push capabilities forward—leveraging foundation models to advance your robotics domain OR bringing your expertise to enhance foundation models themselves. We don't want projects that simply apply existing VLAs to new tasks without advancing the underlying methods.

Participation Agreement

Upon acceptance, participants will sign a participation agreement ensuring that research conducted during the bootcamp will be open-sourced and published collaboratively, benefiting the wider RIG community.
